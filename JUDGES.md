# UNIFIED JUDGING SCORECARD
**AI · Trading & Strategy Track** | Total: **100 pts** (Part A: 50 pts + Part B: 50 pts)

---

## Part A · Mantle general criteria — 50 pts
*Applies to all tracks — scored by all judges*

| Dimension | Points | Key metrics | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technical | 15 | Architecture design, security, code quality, feature completeness | Evaluates code robustness and technical complexity. Core functionality must run end-to-end on the Mantle network. |
| Ecosystem fit | 10 | Mantle stack integration, asset integration (DeFi / CeFi), ecosystem complementarity | Measures how effectively the project leverages Mantle's core features and its strategic contribution to the ecosystem. |
| Business potential | 10 | PMF, tokenomics, viability, GTM strategy | Evaluates sustainable revenue potential, genuine user demand, and a credible post-hackathon go-to-market plan. |
| Innovation | 10 | Originality, differentiated advantage, technical or model breakthrough | Encourages novel ideas and approaches that address real industry pain points — not simple forks or clones. |
| User experience | 5 | UI/UX design, onboarding friction, AA / gasless integration | Focuses on product usability and efforts to lower the barrier for Web2 users entering Web3. |
| **Part A total** | **50** | | |

## Part B · Mantle Network track-specific criteria — 50 pts
*AI & RWA Track — scored by Mantle Network judges*

| Dimension | Points | Description & key evaluation criteria |
|---|---|---|
| AI × RWA integration depth | 15 | Does AI play a substantive, non-cosmetic role in the RWA workflow? Judges look for AI that drives tokenization logic, pricing models, compliance checks, or portfolio decisions — not a chatbot wrapper around a static product. Is the AI output verifiable or auditable? |
| Mantle network integration | 10 | Is Mantle used as a core settlement or execution layer, not just a deployment target? Judges assess whether the project leverages Mantle's low-gas environment, asset infrastructure, or DeFi/CeFi ecosystem in a way that is meaningfully different from deploying on any other chain. |
| Compliance awareness | 10 | Does the project demonstrate awareness of real-world regulatory constraints for the asset class it targets (e.g. KYC/AML, accredited investor rules, jurisdiction-specific requirements)? Bonus for projects that use AI to automate or assist compliance workflows. |
| Track-specific (path A or B) | 10 | **Path A — RWA Infrastructure:** Technical feasibility: Is the asset tokenization flow complete end-to-end (asset identification → on-chain representation → transfer/settlement)? Is the technical approach innovative beyond standard ERC-20/ERC-1400 wrapping? Does the architecture scale to multiple asset classes? **Path B — RWA Application:** Real-world validity: Is the target asset class clearly defined and legally coherent? Are the target users well-specified with genuine demand? Is the end-to-end user experience complete — from asset discovery to on-chain position — without requiring deep Web3 knowledge? |
| Execution & demo quality | 5 | Working prototype or MVP deployed on Mantle; live demo shows a real asset being represented or managed on-chain; open-source repo with clear setup instructions and deployed contract address. |
| **Part B total** | **50** | |

## Grade rubric — full score (Part A + Part B)

| Grade | Total score | Part A — Mantle general indicators | Part B — AI & RWA track-specific indicators |
|---|---|---|---|
| Excellent | 90 – 100 | Breakthrough technical depth; seamless Mantle integration; production-ready with a clear and complete business logic loop. | AI is deeply embedded in the RWA workflow with verifiable outputs; Mantle is used as a strategic layer, not just deployment; compliance posture is thoughtful and realistic; end-to-end demo shows a real asset live on-chain. |
| Good | 70 – 89 | Stable demo with evident innovation; clear Mantle ecosystem value; solid technical implementation. | Clear AI × RWA integration with meaningful Mantle usage; compliance awareness demonstrated; path A or B criteria are substantially met; demo is functional with minor gaps. |

| Poor | 0 – 29 | Concept or slides only; direct plagiarism or fork; non-functional or unable to demonstrate. | No real-world asset involvement; no meaningful AI or Mantle integration; no working demo; concept only with no implementation. |

## Key takeaways

| # | Area | What judges are really looking for |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | AI role | AI must be **functional**, not decorative — it should drive tokenization logic, pricing, compliance, or decisions; not just wrap a static UI. |
| 2 | Mantle usage | Mantle must be a **core execution layer**, not just a deployment target. Show meaningful use of its low-gas environment or DeFi/CeFi ecosystem. |
| 3 | Compliance | Demonstrating **awareness of real-world regulations** (KYC/AML, accredited investor rules) is required. AI-assisted compliance workflows earn bonus points. |
| 4 | End-to-end flow | A complete asset flow — from identification → on-chain representation → settlement/management — is essential for a high score in both paths. |
| 5 | Demo quality | A **live, working demo** deployed on Mantle with open-source code and a deployed contract address is non-negotiable for top marks. |
| 6 | Innovation | Avoid simple forks or clones. Judges reward genuine breakthroughs that solve **real industry pain points** in novel ways. |
| 7 | User experience | Lower the Web3 barrier. Projects that integrate **Account Abstraction or gasless flows** to onboard Web2 users score higher on UX. |
| 8 | Score split | Both parts carry equal weight (50/50). A strong Part A with a weak Part B — or vice versa — **cannot reach the Excellent tier**. |
